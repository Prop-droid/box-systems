Distilled 2026-07-02 from `winners.jsonl` (334 `mb - winner` parent tasks, 2025 S12 to 2026 S24), spend snapshots (parsed for 251/334), and the wiki performance layer. **v2 update 2026-07-04:** added (a) a variant-level mine of the 635 `MB - Winner` subtasks nested in those parents (which hook/image/video variant shipped vs got abandoned), labeled SUB below, and (b) a fresh read-only BigQuery pull from `creative_dashboard` (brand SHA, last 60d to 2026-07-04), labeled BQ60, replacing the stale 2026-06-02 wiki BQ snapshot in patterns 2, 3, 6. Evidence counts are from the archive unless labeled SUB, BQ60, TT (TikTok Shop GMV catalog), or wiki. Read with the caveats at the bottom.

**Why:** winner briefs keep getting written from scratch or from theory. The archive shows the same 14 shapes winning over and over. Briefing against these shapes first is the highest-probability path to the next winner.

**How to apply (global):** when writing any Shameless brief, hook batch, or critique, check the draft against this list. If it matches zero patterns, that is a deliberate exploration bet and should be labeled as one, not the default.

**New global fact (SUB):** across all winners, only 42% of tested variants ship (268 sent to mb vs 367 abandoned). The machine is designed to burn variants. Keep rate by variant format: video 65%, product image 33%, advertorial image 11%. Keep rate by test type: HookTest 71%, Mashup 59%, HeadlineTest 32%, ImageTest 23%, CopyTest 22%. Variant efficiency is drifting down: 2025 keep 45%, 2026 keep 38%. Budget brief volume accordingly: a static image test needs 3 to 4 variants per expected survivor; a hook retro needs barely more than 1.

## 1. Iterate winners, don't hunt fresh concepts
Evidence: 144/334 winners (43%) carry a Retro token; 19 of the top 50 by spend are retros; 12 more carry Proven. SUB: hook retros are the single most efficient test type at variant level (71% of HookTest variants ship vs 23% for ImageTest and 22% for CopyTest).
Apply: default brief = one variable changed on a live winner (image, headline, hook, intro). New concepts are the minority lane, sized accordingly. When choosing which variable, hooks-on-a-proven-body is the cheapest slot machine in the account.

## 2. Static image + headline-stack test is the winner factory
Evidence: 145/334 winners are static or image tests vs 56 video-ish. Test types: ImageTest 89, CopyTest 24, HeadlineTest 16, HookTest 7. Weekly report 2026-06-22: every winner but one was a static. BQ60: statics did $469k at 0.86 ROAS vs video $530k at 0.68 over the last 60d, so statics remain the efficiency lane even though video now takes slightly more spend. Awareness-formula slice on statics (BQ60 ai_formula): Solution Aware 1.02 at $86k and Most Aware 0.98 at $115k carry the account; Problem Aware runs 0.56 at $83k.
SUB nuance: only 33% of static variants ship (vs 65% of video variants). That is not a weakness, it is the design: statics are cheap enough to burn 3 abandons per survivor, video variants only get subtasked once already curated.
Apply: the standard winner machine is 10 headlines applied to a proven layout (Problem-Headline-Benefit). Ship these weekly before commissioning video, and brief static batches at 3 to 4 variants per survivor you actually need. Skew the stack toward solution-aware and most-aware framings; problem-aware headline stacks are where the 0.56 dead weight lives.

## 3. Offer + urgency wrapper is the biggest dollar lane
Evidence: 80 winners, $114k tracked spend, the largest cluster. Top single winner: NewOffer4FREEGifts at $36k. Recurring brief headlines: "UP TO 46% OFF + FREE GIFTS", "JUST $2.69 PER BAG", "SALE IS ENDING TONIGHT". BQ60 reweight (was the 2026-06-02 wiki snapshot): Scarcity and Urgency now 1.10 at $77k and trending up (1.00 prior 30d, 1.21 recent 30d); Direct Offer softened to 0.94 at $53k (1.03 prior, 0.92 recent); two lanes the old snapshot missed are now co-leaders: USP 1.10 at $58k (stable) and Comparison 1.05, plus Simple Product as the biggest tagged lane at $135k, 0.82 lifetime but 1.11 in the recent 30d. Bare Curiosity still trails at 0.56.
SUB nuance: the offer lane wins by volume, not hit rate. Offer-urgency static variants ship at only 21% (31/145) and offer advertorial images at 14%, vs fiber statics 42% and GLP1 statics 48%. Offer-urgency VIDEO variants ship at 73%, the wrapper travels better on video than the static grind suggests.
Apply: wrap every angle in a concrete offer (46% off + 4 free gifts, $2.69/bag) plus a deadline; scarcity framing currently beats plain direct-offer framing. But budget the static offer lane like a lottery (5 variants per survivor) and treat USP and product-forward statics as equal-priority wrappers, not fallbacks.

## 4. Reason-why sale narrative beats naked discounts
Evidence: WeAreSorry letter 14 winners, Warehouse/Packing 5, tariff price-cut 3. "WE'RE SAYING GOODBYE" appears in 13 briefs, "LAST 2 PALLETS SELLING OUT" in 7.
SUB warning: reason-why STATIC variants now ship at only 14% (4/29), the worst substantive lane in the archive, while reason-why VIDEO variants ship at 57% (8/14). The letter/apology excuse still works read aloud; as a static reskin it is nearly exhausted.
Apply: give the discount a story (apology letter, warehouse clearing, tariffs, goodbye). The excuse makes the scarcity believable; refresh the excuse, keep the mechanic, and put fresh excuses into video/CTV letter reads first rather than another static pass.

## 5. Fiber-shock comparison headline
Evidence: 75 fiber-angle winners, $58k tracked. "MORE FIBER THAN 3 CUPS OF BROCCOLI" recurs 8x in briefs; FiberThere'sNoExcuse is the #2 all-time spender ($26k). "Same bag, different math" opener on 10 winners. SUB: fiber is the healthiest big static lane at variant level (42% of 107 static variants ship, double the offer lane) and fiber video variants ship at 65%. BQ60: Comparison-classified statics run 1.05, 1.20 in the recent 30d.
Apply: lead statics with fiber quantified against a known food (broccoli cups, bowls of oatmeal). The number vs candy is the hook; keep 26g canonical.

## 6. Body-function disruption hook, offer-wrapped on paid
Evidence: every top-5 GMV TikTok video uses a poop/body beat (TT: "Can't poop? Eat this candy" $23.6k GMV). "WHEN POOP FEELS LIKE A BRICK" recurs in briefs; SH-13107 Poop Retro ran 1.18 ROAS (wiki weekly) and its GLP1 headline-test variant is still a top-15 spender at 1.40 in the current window (BQ60). BQ60 reweight: bare Problem-Solution is 0.59 at $85k lifetime-window (was 0.62 in the 2026-06-02 snapshot), 0.65 in the recent 30d; the Problem Aware formula slice runs 0.56. SUB: poop-body static variants ship at 47%, well above the 33% static average, so the disruption beat earns its test slots.
Apply: disruption beat goes first, stat stack third. On Meta paid, pair it with the offer wrapper (pattern 3) or a scarcity frame or it wins attention and loses money; the fresh pull says un-wrapped problem framing still runs ~0.56 to 0.65.

## 7. Toxic-relationship breakup copy frame
Evidence: the single most reused winner primary text: "Been in a toxic relationship with your sweet tooth?" on 51 winners, plus 17 more on the new-year variant. Goodbye Letter script ("I ghosted Metamucil") extends it to the supplement enemy.
Apply: frame the switch as a breakup (with sugar, with fiber powder, with empty calories). Proven primary text; iterate the second line, keep the frame.

## 8. QVC / live-shopping excerpt format
Evidence: 18 winners, $42k tracked; the single QVC testimonial video is #3 all-time ($21k); QVC hook-test retro added $8k. Leaderboard: 0.88 ROAS at $69k spend. SUB: QVC video variants ship at 60% (18/30), consistent with the video-lane average.

## 9. Founder personal-stakes VSL
Evidence: Genetics 1.31 ROAS at $116k and Dad Bod 1.19 at $38k are the two best scripts on the leaderboard (wiki). Motion benchmark: confession hook is top tier on hit rate and spend use. SUB: only 9 founder-VSL variants exist at subtask level (4 shipped), so this lane is under-tested relative to its leaderboard rank.
Apply: founder-voice with personal stakes (genetics, dad bod) outranks brand story. Open on confession, route mechanism through fiber. The variant pipeline is thin here; commissioning more founder hook retros is cheap headroom.

## 10. The 7-beat winning structure is load-bearing
Evidence: constant across top scripts (wiki leaderboard): hook, tension, in-narrative product reveal, stat stack (26g fiber / 70 cal / 3g sugar / 3g net carbs), mechanism, social proof, CTA. Missing reveal, stats, or CTA correlates with lower ROAS; TT catalog shows the same 5-beat arc on every video over $3k GMV.
Apply: audit every script against the beats before shipping; the stat stack is non-negotiable and verbatim.

## 11. GLP-1 consequence lane, compliance-safe
Evidence: 31 GLP1/weight-loss winners; the dedicated glp-1 LP serves 39 winners; "26g of Fiber for GLP-1 Support" headline on 5. SUB: GLP1/WL static variants ship at 48%, the best static keep rate of any named angle.
Apply: address the audience via the consequence (fiber deficit, cravings, constipation) not the drug. GLP-1 as category term only, never drug names, per compliance-guardrails.

## 12. Mom-creator rambling UGC archetype (whitelisting)
Evidence: 50 UGC/TikTok-lane winners incl. 18 WL tokens; monthly Tiktok_UGC batches keep graduating. Steph Joplin car-line ad (2:24, slow hook, hedged CTA) won while breaking every UGC heuristic. SUB upgrade: whitelisting variants ship at 83% (35/42), the highest keep rate of ANY lane in the archive; Tiktok_UGC variants ship at 62%.
Apply: for WL assets, cast the archetype (mom, kitchen, family occasions, unpolished) and weight performance over structure. Tighten-the-hook edits are challenger tests, not fixes. At an 83% keep rate, whitelisting is the closest thing the account has to a sure-thing variant; the constraint is creator supply, not test budget.

## 13. Social proof via Amazon reviews and retail sellout
Evidence: "Almost four thousand glowing Amazon reviews" opener on 12 winners; "sold out at Target for the 3rd time" on 10; IABSOLUTELLYLOVE review-verbatim statics: 8 winners.
SUB warning: social-proof STATIC variants shipped 0 of 13 in the archive mine, while social-proof video variants shipped 9 of 12 (75%). The review beat still converts spoken; the review-verbatim static lane looks exhausted as-is.
Apply: cite countable proof (review count, Amazon rank, Target sellouts) as the trust beat, primarily inside video. Re-earn the static lane with a new visual treatment before spending more test slots on review-verbatim layouts.

## 14. Seasonal moment retro
Evidence: 26 seasonal winners (NewYearNewMe 9, Christmas, Carnival, fall sale opener on 48). These are mostly retros of proven creative reskinned to the moment. SUB: seasonal static variants ship at only 19%; seasonal video variants at 50%.
Apply: calendar the reskins (NYNM, Halloween, BFCM, Valentines) 2 sprints ahead; swap wrapper and imagery, keep the proven body. Expect static reskins to burn 4 to 5 variants per survivor; lean on the video cut of each seasonal moment.

## Caveats
- "Winner" = graduated ClickUp status (spend threshold), not profitability. Blended 60d platform ROAS is ~0.77 (BQ60); the June cmROAS 0.57 figure in v1 is a margin-adjusted metric, a different axis. These still rank relative winners.
- SUB semantics: variant "shipped" = subtask status `sent to mb`; "abandoned" = status `abandoned`, which mixes never-launched variants with launched-then-killed ones (289/367 abandoned subtasks carry live meta ad ids, so most were launched). Keep rate reads as "survived the test", not "never ran".
- SUB angle classification is regex over parent task names (same token families as v1); 94 variants under unclassified parents (47% keep) are excluded from per-angle claims. A parent matching two token families counts in both.
- BQ60: `ai_angle`/`ai_formula`/`headline_type` are populated ONLY on IMAGE rows; all $530k of 60d VIDEO spend is angle-untagged in BQ. Every per-angle ROAS figure above is statics-only; video angle claims rest on the archive/TT/wiki layers.
- Spend parsed for 251/334 records; 83 have no snapshot, so evidence counts are firmer than dollar totals.
- 2026 mix is shifting: offer-lane share of winners fell (55/201 in 2025 vs 20/129 in 2026) while fiber held, and variant keep rate fell 45% to 38%; re-run this distill (including the SUB mine) after H2.
