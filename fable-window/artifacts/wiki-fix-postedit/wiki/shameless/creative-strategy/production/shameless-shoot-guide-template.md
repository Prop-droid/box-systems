## How to use this template

Each shoot cycle, make a copy and update three sections:

1. **Script 1** and **Script 2** — cycle-specific hooks + body, reviewed against [[compliance-guardrails]] and the script writing rules in [[video-structure#script-writing-rules]].
2. **Shotlist** — keep the ~11 core product/lifestyle clips, swap in 3–5 fresh creative shots per cycle so Meta keeps seeing new signals.
3. **Experiment Wishlist** — rotate the seed ideas so talent keeps feeling invited to try different angles.

Everything else (technical setup, deliverables, rejection criteria, opener, closer) is stable. Don't rewrite it each cycle — it's load-bearing.

Pairs with:
- [[shameless-talent-production-standards]] — production/delivery standards (lighting, format, reactions, rejection)
- OG Snacks DTC Creator Brief in Notion — messaging strategy (hooks, story arc, authority)

## What we're making

We're shooting fast-paced UGC that feels like you're FaceTiming a friend about something you just discovered. Not polished. Not a commercial. **Reaction-first, brand-second.**

What we care about most:

1. **Your real reaction** when you taste it. Big, unfiltered, slightly embarrassing. Viewers can tell the difference between "nodded and smiled" and "genuinely surprised."
2. **Visual variety.** Different outfits, different rooms, different angles. If every clip looks the same, Meta can't learn anything new.
3. **Creative risks.** We've seen 300 kitchen-counter talking heads. If you want to try an unexpected angle, POV, or location, *please do.* See the Experiment Wishlist at the bottom.

---

## Technical setup

1. **Natural light only.** Face the window. No ring lights, no overhead kitchen lights. See the [lighting checklist](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1ByePSEZiSVGUDfyXTdGUwkF0EB2HjTG_hG7bmnE2BzI/edit?usp=sharing).
2. **Rear camera** of your phone. Main lens.
3. **Vertical 9x16**, at least 1080p.
4. **Framing.** Don't sit too far from the camera. [Bad example for reference](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1-KANvaSs4S9LWu8Foy8cFJ9YvIrkLaht/view?usp=sharing).
5. **Quiet room.** No fridge hum, washing machine, TV in background.
6. **Teleprompter is fine** if it helps you land the lines, as long as it still sounds natural. [iPhone app](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/teleprompter/id941620509) / [Android tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4dGK-Y44WAk).
7. **No in-app shooting.** Don't use Instagram, TikTok, CapCut, etc. No captions, filters, music, or transitions — we edit all of that.

## Deliverables checklist

You'll send back a total of **16 raw files**:

- **2 Testimonial videos** (one per script, each in one continuous take)
- **2 Alt-hook videos** (just the opening hook line of each script, shot with a different opener — for split-testing)
- **12 B-roll clips** (one per row in the shotlist below)

Rename every file to match its label: `Script1_Full`, `Script1_AltHook`, `Script2_Full`, `Script2_AltHook`, `Clip1`, `Clip2`, ... `Clip12`.

## Experiment Wishlist (bring us 1–2 of your own)

On top of the shotlist above, please shoot **1 or 2 of your own ideas** we didn't ask for. Anything visually unexpected. Some seeds if you need them:

- **Unexpected POV** — shot from the floor looking up, from inside a drawer, from the dog's level, from above.
- **Location swap** — car cupholder, hotel bed, airport gate, gym bag, grocery cart, nightstand, desk while "working."
- **"Caught snacking"** — over-the-shoulder angle of you eating like the camera wasn't supposed to be there.
- **Phone screen framing** — screen-record yourself reacting to the nutrition label, scrolling the reviews, or replying to a fake DM.
- **Before / after cut** — same spot, two outfits, two energies. A hard jump cut between "before fiber" and "after fiber."
- **Confession whisper** — lean close to the lens and confess something quietly about the whole bag.

Rule: the visual idea has to *mean* something. A weird angle for no reason is just noise. The angle should serve the moment.

## What gets rejected

- Face in heavy shadow, backlit, or half-lit
- Ring light or overhead kitchen light visible in the eyes
- No on-camera eat, or a flat / polite reaction
- No verbal CTA on the testimonial takes
- Videos that sound like a commercial — if it sounds like you're reading a brand line, rewrite in your own words
- Files named incorrectly (we match files to clip numbers — unnamed files slow everything down)

---

**Pacing:** fast. **Attitude:** like you just discovered this five minutes ago and can't shut up about it. **Pride:** leave it at the door. The more you're willing to look slightly unhinged on camera, the better these perform.

We'd rather cut from abundance than reshoot. Shoot extra takes. Try the weird angle. Go bigger than feels natural.
