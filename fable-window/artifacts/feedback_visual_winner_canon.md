# Visual winner canon: what winning Shameless statics LOOK like

Basis: read the actual creative images of the top 32 static/image winners by lifetime spend from winners.jsonl (334 winners, 192 static-classified, 151 with a resolvable image). 3 turned out to be video frames and were excluded; 29 statics analyzed, 28 unique creatives, roughly $110k combined spend. Full source list in visual-canon.SOURCES.md. Closes the visual gap declared by tasks 03/20.

**Why:** Tasks 03 and 20 mined names, copy, and win rates but nobody had looked at the pixels. Brief instructions like "make a static" underdetermine output; these are the proven visual shapes to brief against.

**How to apply:** When briefing ImageTest/static tasks, name one of the six families below instead of describing from scratch. Reuse the recurring elements (banner sandwich, dashed table, hand-drawn arrow + CTA chip, badge cluster) as concrete Figma-level instructions.

---

## Pattern 1: Urgency banner sandwich over product tableau (8 winners, ~$33.5k, incl. the #2 spender at $14.8k)

Full-width flat color banner pair (yellow with black condensed caps + red or magenta strip with contrasting caps) pinned to top or bottom edge. Under it, a physical product tableau: 3 to 4 retail boxes or bags stacked pyramid-style on a photoreal kitchen counter, warehouse, or lifestyle scene (3D render look). Headlines are scarcity lines: "LAST CALL TO BUY / LAST 2 PALLETS SELLING OUT", "ONCE THEY'RE GONE... THEY'RE GONE", "LIMITED SUPPLY ALERT". Packaging carries all nutrition claims; the overlay only sells urgency. Banner at top in 6 of 8; one seasonal variant swaps the counter for a themed scene (Halloween graveyard, zombie hand holding the bag). Evidence: SH-187, SH-2134, SH-803, SH-1936, SH-438, SH-3030, SH-4659, SH-4749.

## Pattern 2: Us-vs-them comparison table (3 winners, ~$19.1k, incl. the #1 spender at $15.3k)

Bold condensed black or white italic caps headline top center ("MORE FIBER THAN 3 CUPS OF BROCCOLI. STILL TASTES LIKE CANDY.", "THIS ISN'T YOUR KID'S CANDY"). Two loose gummy piles side by side (ours vs Sour Patch Kids, both labeled 50g), then 5 dashed-divider rows: calories, net carbs, sugar, fiber, and a joke row ("Tastes Great (For Real)" vs "(But at what cost)"). Logo bottom center. Background either soft blue-to-cream vertical gradient or dark purple/orange smoke. Highest text density of any family (~40 words) and it wins anyway; the table format earns the reading. Evidence: SH-972, SH-128, SH-3915.

## Pattern 3: Macro appetite close-up, full bleed (2 winners, ~$22.7k, best per-ad spend)

Sugared gummies fill 100 percent of the frame in extreme close-up (crystals visible). Translucent white horizontal strips top and bottom carry the only text: short headline up top ("FALL SPECIAL SALE", "Amazon's Sugar-Craving Killer"), offer bar bottom ("UP TO 46% OFF + FREE GIFTS", "$2.69 PER BAG"). Optionally one thin 3-column benefit strip. No packaging, no logo block, under 15 words total. Pure appetite appeal with a price frame. Evidence: SH-2132, SH-7474.

## Pattern 4: Broccoli mechanism series, minimal product-only (4 winners, ~$12k)

The "More fiber than 3 cups of broccoli. Still tastes like candy." claim rendered visually. Hero gummy or bag shot with real broccoli as prop or implied comparison; one used a magnifying glass revealing glowing fiber veins inside the gummy (mechanism visualization). Two signature elements repeat across all four: a hand-drawn white or red arrow pointing at the product, and a small solid-color CTA chip reading "Get Your 29g of Fiber". Backgrounds: flat pastel pink, blurred green, or warm wood. 10 to 20 words. Evidence: SH-969, SH-1500, SH-2123, SH-2808.

## Pattern 5: DR clutter bag hero with badge cluster and price anchor (4 winners, ~$10.6k)

Single bag hero at center-right, angled. Around it: 3 to 5 benefit pills or gradient circles ("29G FIBER", "92% Less Sugar", "Gut Health Hero", "Blood Sugar Safe"), a starburst discount badge ("Up to 41% OFF", "46% OFF +4 FREE GIFTS"), and a Was/Now price anchor with strikethrough ("Was $4.90, NOW $2.89 PER BAG"). Headline is a callout or promise in heavy caps, top-left ("HELPS LOSE WEIGHT", "MOMS! YOU WON'T NEED TO DIET AGAIN", "AT LAST! A CANDY THAT'S GOOD FOR YOUR GUT HEALTH"). Backgrounds vary from cream with organic blobs to full neon arcade. Deliberately busy, classic ugly-ad energy. Evidence: SH-889, SH-1479, SH-3029, SH-2389.

## Pattern 6: Premium lifestyle product shot, serif headline (4 winner tasks, 3 unique creatives, ~$6.9k)

One bag styled like an editorial food shot with the real fruit scattered around it (raspberries on a glass pedestal, cherries on mountain rocks, berries on marble counter). Headline in elegant serif italic, mixed weight: "26g of fiber never tasted like this." plus red subline "Shamelessly sweet. Surprisingly functional." Executions range from clean cream background to color-blocked red/yellow banner sandwich around the same photo. Same copy tested across at least 3 distinct visual treatments and won repeatedly; the claim carried the series. Evidence: SH-920, SH-1541 (same creative rerun), SH-1557, SH-1558.

## One-off winners worth iterating (3 winners, ~$7k)

- Flavor grid: 6 bags in a 2x3 grid on flat cyan, gummy piles behind each bag, one gag flavor planted ("Pneumatic Tire Treat") under the headline "Guess which flavor isn't ours? 10+ flavors with 26g fiber." Curiosity + range in one frame. (SH-11295, $4.0k)
- UGC haul photo: ~40 bags fanned in color rows in front of a shipping box on a wooden table, natural window light, zero text overlay. Reads as a customer photo, not an ad. (SH-13005, $1.6k)
- Results timeline: "After 3 days / After 14 days / After 30 days" ladder where the product bags themselves multiply per row (2, then 4, then 6). Progress narrative without faces or before/after claims. (SH-3911, $1.5k)

## Cross-cutting rules (counts out of 28 unique statics)

- Zero human faces. Not one winning static contains a person or body part except the Halloween zombie hand. People only appear in video winners.
- Headline at top edge in 24 of 28. Bottom-banner placement appeared only in the tableau family.
- Headline typography splits three ways: heavy condensed caps black-on-yellow or white-on-color (urgency/DR, 15), chunky rounded sans with outline (playful claims, 6), serif italic (premium family, 4), handwritten casual (2).
- Packaging visible in ~20 of 28; the bag itself is the main claim carrier (3G sugar / 70 cal / 3G net carbs dots readable in nearly every packaging shot). When packaging is absent (macro, comparison piles) claims move into the headline or table.
- Discount badge or offer strip in 15 of 28. Always "Up to 41%" or "Up to 46% OFF", often "+ FREE GIFTS" or "+4 FREE GIFTS". Was $4.90 / Now $2.89 or $2.69 strikethrough anchor in 4.
- The broccoli fiber comparison appears in 5 of 28 creatives across two different families; it is the single most reused visual claim.
- Hand-drawn arrows (pointing at product or CTA) in 5; they cluster in the winning minimal/casual families.
- Color fields: flavor-coded brand palette does the work (purple, green, blue, red, teal bags); backgrounds are either flat saturated fields (cyan, pink, lavender), soft blue-to-cream gradients, or photoreal counters. Yellow + red is reserved for urgency banners.
- Text density has two winning modes and no middle: under 15 words (macro, minimal, haul) or 30 plus words in structured form (comparison table, badge cluster). Unstructured mid-length paragraphs never appear.
- Render style splits roughly half photoreal 3D product render (tableaus, neon, Halloween), half real photography (pedestal, marble, haul, macro). Both win; renders dominate the urgency family, photos dominate premium/UGC.

**Caveat:** counts are from the top-spend slice with locally resolvable images (28 unique of 151 candidates), so families that ran early 2025 before thumbnail caching may be undercounted. Advertorial statics and Amazon PT images are not represented. Related: [[feedback-winner-patterns-2026h1]], [[concepts/winning-creative-resolution]].
