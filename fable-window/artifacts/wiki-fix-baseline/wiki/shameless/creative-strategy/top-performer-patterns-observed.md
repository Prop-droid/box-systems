# Top-Performer Patterns Observed — Shameless Snacks

**Summary**: Synthesis of recurring hook shapes, transcript arcs, and compliance-edge moves across the 17 analyst-scored Cruva winners. Use as a feed for `shameless-script` and `hook-batch` skills, and as a reference when critiquing new drafts.

**Sources**: [[top-performer-hooks-scripts-catalog]] (17 videos, $166 to $23,611 GMV, all-time TikTok Shop).

**Last updated**: 2026-06-02 (added Meta paid ROAS cross-check). Prior: 2026-05-08.

---

## Hook patterns ranked by analyst score and dollar performance

### 1. Body-function disruption (9-10 scoring, top-3 GMV slot)

The single most predictive hook shape across the catalog. Every video in the top 5 lifetime GMV uses a bodily-function or consequence beat.

- "Can't poop? Eat this candy." — kelseyyyshops, $23,611
- "I was on the toilet for 26 minutes." — rickeyeugenebrown, $7,216
- "Diarrhea Alert!" — blackfridaybrian, $217
- "any food noise you have will be destroyed" — thesnasan, $166

The hook shape works because it weaponizes the approved language ([[shameless-snacks#allowed-language]]: "pooping every day", "food noise") and creates an immediate curiosity gap a viewer cannot resolve without watching. Polite reformulations of the same benefit (#10 calibrokenbrain, "29g Fiber 3g Sugar") earn 1/30th the GMV.

**Use case**: when writing UGC or founder confession openings, the disruption beat goes first. Reserve the stat stack for slot 3-4.

### 2. Same-calorie contrast reveal (9 scoring)

Two-bowl visual reveal where same-calorie commodity candy is shown next to Shameless. This is the only structural pattern from the catalog that matched body-function for top-tier performance ($20,351, crys1216).

The script formula: "Same calories. Wild difference." then "This is X calories worth of Y" then the sugar/fiber gap. The hook is the LACK of difference (calories) followed by the reveal of difference (sugar, fiber).

**Use case**: pure narrator-and-product format with hands and bowls. No talking head needed. Pairs well with Sour Patch Kids, Haribo, generic gummy as the comparison.

### 3. Stat-shock blurt (8-9 scoring, mid-tier GMV)

Direct numerical claim with no setup, no preamble.

- "Holy fibre. There's 26 grams in a bag." — courtneystager, $8,443
- "This candy has more fibre than sugar." — allie.cat12, $3,846
- "29g Fiber 3g Sugar" — calibrokenbrain, $570

When this works it works because the number itself is the surprise (26g fiber in candy is genuinely unexpected). When it underperforms, it is because the creator dropped the stat as a label rather than a reaction. "Holy fibre" wins, "29g Fiber 3g Sugar" does not.

**Use case**: text overlay first frame, then VO that reacts to the stat instead of stating it.

### 4. Curiosity-gap permission ("Nobody talks about this...")

Open loop with implied insider knowledge.

- "Nobody talks about this..." — chiefgrunto, $7,229
- "Don't make this mistake when eating these." — rickeyeugenebrown, $7,216

Both pivot to the body-function reveal mid-video. This pattern is mostly useful as a setup beat that earns the second-surprise pivot, not as a standalone hook.

### 5. Demographic callout (lowest tier in this catalog)

- "Sour Candy Lovers" — charleneshares, $244
- "so good when you're on a weight loss journey" — disneygirl73, $186

These hooks underperform across the catalog. The demographic callout works in batch tests when the audience is highly specific (per [[hook-framework]] and the existing [[hook-batch]] skill taxonomy), but in this dataset the hooks read as generic and the videos lack the disruption beat to compensate.

## The Golden Script arc, observed

Every transcript over $3,000 GMV in the catalog follows the same five-beat structure:

1. **Disruption hook** (1-2 sentences, body-function or contrast)
2. **Brand drop** ("These are Shameless Snacks" / "It's Shameless Snacks")
3. **Stat stack** (26g fiber, 70-90 cal, 3g sugar, 3g net carbs, in that order or near it)
4. **Variety or texture beat** (flavors listed, "they nice and thick", "you can eat the whole bag")
5. **Soft CTA or humor close** ("don't eat the whole bag in one sitting", "link below", coupon mention)

The lowest performers in the catalog skip beat 1 (open with brand drop) or skip beat 5 (close on stats with no warmth). See @disneygirl73 ($186) for both failures.

## Second-surprise pattern (the "however")

Roughly half of the top-tier transcripts pivot at the 60% mark with a "however," "but," or "the part nobody talks about" beat that reveals a consequence or unexpected upside.

- chiefgrunto: "however, there is something that like nobody talks about. These things are loaded with fiber..."
- rickeyeugenebrown: entire video IS the second-surprise structure, taste hook leading to fiber-consequence reveal
- thesnasan: "The best part is that this is 26 grams of fiber"

The second surprise is what graduates a stat-stack ad into a story. This is the lever to pull when first-surprise hooks alone are not converting.

## Compliance-edge observation

The body-function hooks are the highest performers AND the closest to the compliance line. Per [[shameless-snacks#allowed-language]] and the project memory log, "pooping every day", "food noise", and similar bodily-regularity phrasing are approved at Shameless account level. Disease and treatment language remains off-limits.

The catalog confirms the approved-language bet: every top-tier hook uses words that would have been off-limits at most other supplement or food brands. Killing those phrases would kill the top performers. New scripts should default to using the approved language unless there is a specific compliance reason not to.

## Recurring stat phrasing (canonical, observed in the wild)

The exact language creators reach for when stating the brand canon (per memory [[project_shameless_canonical_stats]]):

- "26 grams of fiber" — most common, 10+ videos
- "70 calories" or "70 to 90 calories" — 8+ videos
- "3 grams of sugar" — 9+ videos
- "3 net carbs" or "3 grams of carbs" — 5+ videos
- "macro-friendly" — 3+ videos
- "keto-friendly and gluten-free" — 4+ videos
- "guilt-free" or "without the guilt" — 5+ videos

When generating UGC scripts, these are the phrasings real creators land on. Use them verbatim. The minor stat slip ("90 calories" instead of "70 calories", "29g Fiber" instead of "26g") that appears in two of the lower-tier videos is a quality issue worth flagging in critique, not adopting.

## Tone and format distribution

**Tone (n=17)**: casual 13, humorous 3, educational 1. Humor wraps the body-function angle 3-for-3. Educational is the comparison-reveal format.

**Integration type**: review 12, unboxing 4, other 1.

**Face visible**: yes 14, no 3. The face-not-visible videos still won when the hands-and-product framing carried the contrast reveal. Face is not load-bearing.

## What this catalog does NOT cover

- Video-level visual production (lighting, editing pace, B-roll cuts) is not in the data, only on-screen-text and product-screen-time fields. Cruva analyst tagging is text-first.
- Awareness levels, persona segmentation, and angle taxonomy are richer in [[hook-framework]] and [[creative-angles]] than in this raw winner catalog. The patterns above complement those theory pages with revenue-validated data.
- Pre-Aug-2025 winners are missing from the data (Cruva returned no analyzed entries before that window). Older winners would broaden the pattern set if/when pagination escalation lands.

## Meta paid ROAS cross-check (2026-06-02, BigQuery)

The patterns above are ranked on **TikTok Shop GMV**. On **Meta paid** (a different surface, last 60d, `creative_dashboard`) the dollar signal lands on a different axis: offer and urgency, not hook shape.

- Profitable at scale: Direct Offer (ROAS 1.04, $57k), Scarcity & Urgency (1.01, $118k), USP (1.00, $71k).
- Losing money despite spend: Problem-Solution (0.62, $121k), Curiosity-Driven (0.50, $35k), Testimonial (0.52), Comparison (0.52).
- Static images hold most top-ROAS slots on Meta.

Reconciliation: the body-function-disruption hook still wins attention on TikTok GMV, but on Meta paid it only converts profitably when wrapped in an offer or urgency frame. A strong hook on a bare problem-led ad is a 0.5 to 0.6 ROAS lane right now. Pair hook strength with offer or urgency structure, not problem-agitation alone.
