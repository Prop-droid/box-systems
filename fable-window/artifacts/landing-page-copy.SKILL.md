# Landing Page Copy

DR landing-page copy for any brand. Module-by-module hand-off shape, plaintext only, internal critique on first drafts. Brand canon supplied per task.

## When to invoke

Any LP / sales-page / advertorial / PDP / hero-section copy request. Includes ATF rewrites, full-page rewrites, individual module rewrites (just the H1, just the FAQ, just the proof block), and competitor-LP adaptations.

For Shameless-specific LP copy, layer Shameless brand canon on top of this skill (26g/70/3/3 stats, daily-fiber CTA convention, allowed-language list).

## The deliverable shape (DEFAULT, full LP)

For a full LP first draft, the output is the following modules **in this exact order**, each separated by a blank line. No section dividers, no `## H1` markdown headers — just the module name on its own line followed by the prose.

1. **H1 (5 numbered options)** — `1.` through `5.`, no header, no "(pick one)". Each H1 tests a distinct angle (stat shock, benefit promise, demographic callout, contrast/comparison, problem-named).
2. **Subhead (1 line)** — the sentence under the chosen H1. Doesn't repeat the H1; cashes its check or adds the proof beat.
3. **Value-prop prose (3-5 sentences)** — what it does + who it's for + why it's different. One paragraph, no bullets unless the user asks.
4. **Bulleted benefit list (3-5 bullets)** — each bullet starts with a benefit verb, ends with a specific. *"Replaces your fiber supplement. 26g per bag, no powder."*
5. **Proof block** — one of: 3 short testimonials (10-15 words each), or stat array (3-5 numbers with one-line context each), or press logos with one-line callout, or before/after framing if brand-approved.
6. **FAQ (3-5 questions)** — real objections in customer language, one short paragraph answer each. No "What is X?" filler — only objections that actually block the purchase.
7. **Primary CTA (1 line)** — one ask, one verb, one link target, named clearly.
8. **Secondary CTA (1 line, optional)** — for the visitor not ready to convert. Often a quiz, a guide, or a soft email signup. Skip if the brand wants a single-CTA page.

Plaintext. **No triple-backtick fences ever.** Module name (e.g. `H1`) appears on its own line as a label, followed by the content. No bold headers, no markdown `#`, no boxed callouts. No em dashes anywhere in the copy.

## When the user asks for just one module

Drop the full structure. Output only what they asked for, in the same plaintext discipline (e.g. "give me the H1" → 5 numbered options, no preamble, no closing).

## H1 craft (the 5)

The H1 is the headline that earns the next 5 seconds of scroll. Apply the `micro-scripts` skill — best H1s are A/B equation, stark reminder, or whole-micro-story. Each of the 5 tests a different angle:

- **Stat shock** — *"26 grams of fiber. In a bag of candy."*
- **Benefit promise** — *"Eat candy. Get your fiber. Poop every day."*
- **Demographic callout** — *"For women in perimenopause who still want dessert."*
- **Contrast / comparison** — *"More fiber than a bowl of oatmeal. Tastes like Sour Patch."*
- **Problem-named** — *"Fiber supplements are a chore. We made one you'll actually finish."*

Avoid corporate H1s: *"The future of fiber"*, *"Wellness, reimagined"*, *"Snacking with purpose"*. Generic benefit-claims that could belong to any brand.

## Subhead craft

The subhead is the second hook. Use it to:

- Land the proof the H1 implied (H1 = stat, subhead = the why)
- Add the demographic specifier (H1 = benefit, subhead = "for [audience]")
- Cash the curiosity check (H1 = comparison, subhead = the mechanism)

Avoid: paraphrasing the H1, marketing taglines, "Now available", "Order today".

## Value-prop prose

3-5 sentences, one paragraph. Cover, in order:

1. **What it does** — one sentence, concrete.
2. **Who it's for** — the buyer in their words, not yours.
3. **Why it's different** — the DSI (per `micro-scripts`): superlative + important + believable + measurable + own-able.
4. **What changes for the buyer** — the after-state, in sensory terms.

Avoid: brand origin stories on the LP (those go on /about), founder bio in the value prop, jargon, "passion", "dedication", "excellence".

## Benefit bullets

3-5 bullets, each starting with a benefit verb (*Replace, Skip, Eat, Get, Stop, Start, Trade*). End each with a **specific number, name, or measurement**.

- ✅ *Replace your fiber powder: 26g per bag, no spoon, no water.*
- ❌ *High in fiber and tastes great* (no specific, no verb, generic)
- ✅ *Eat the whole bag. Only 70 calories, only 3g of sugar.*
- ❌ *Healthy snacking made delicious* (corporate filler)

## Proof block

Pick the format that fits the brand and the page goal:

- **Testimonial array (3 short)** — 10-15 words each, in voice. Single name + role/age. Cuts longer reviews to the dramatic essence.
- **Stat array (3-5 numbers)** — *"26g fiber. 70 calories. 3g sugar. 3g net carbs."* Numbers do the lifting.
- **Press / logo strip** — one line of callouts: *"Featured in Vogue, The Strategist, Well+Good"*. Skip unless the press is real and recognizable.
- **Before/after** — only if brand-approved. Use behavior change (*"Used to take psyllium husk twice a day. Now I just eat candy."*), not body change.

One proof format per LP unless it's a long-form sales letter.

## FAQ craft

The FAQ is where buyers get past the last 3 objections before checkout. Real questions in real language. No "What is X?" filler — only what blocks the click.

Common DR objection categories (pick the 3-5 that matter most):

- **Price** — *"Why is it more expensive than [comparable]?"*
- **Sufficiency** — *"Is one bag enough fiber per day?"*
- **Compatibility** — *"Can I eat this if I'm [diet]?"*
- **Trust** — *"What if I don't like it?"*
- **Mechanism** — *"How does it have so much fiber and so little sugar?"*
- **Frequency** — *"How often should I eat it?"*

Answers are 1-3 sentences each. No "Great question!", no preamble, no FAQ-language ("We're glad you asked"). Land the answer fast.

## CTA discipline

Primary CTA: one verb, one object, one link target, named clearly.

- ✅ *Eat the whole bag → eatshameless.com*
- ✅ *Start with one bag a day → /shop*
- ❌ *Click here* / *Learn more* / *Shop now* (verbless, vague)

If the brand has a habit-driver convention (*"make it your daily fiber"*), use it verbatim.

Secondary CTA (optional): for the not-ready visitor. *"Not ready to commit? Take the 30-second fiber quiz."* Skip if the brand wants a single-CTA page.

## Brand canon — supplied per task

This skill is brand-agnostic. Expect the user (or brief) to supply:

- Stats / numbers that must appear or are off-limits
- Allowed language (terms approved at account level)
- Off-limits language (default off-limits: disease verbs, drug-name adjacency, body-transformation framing, weight-loss audience triggers — unless brand-approved)
- CTA convention (habit-driver vs offer-led vs subscription-led)
- Voice rules
- Page goal (cold traffic acquisition vs warm lifecycle vs PDP vs advertorial — these have different module priorities)

If ambiguous, ask **one** clarifying question via `CLARIFICATION_REQUIRED` before drafting. Page goal is the highest-leverage unknown: if the user hasn't named it and it can't be inferred from the brief, that's the one question to ask. Otherwise draft with marked placeholders and say so after the CTA.

## Internal critique — ordered checklist with kill-criteria (silent)

After drafting, run this pass **internally**, in this order, and fix in place before printing. Each step has a kill-criterion: fail it and the module gets rewritten — not annotated, not shipped with a caveat.

0. **Canon/compliance gate.** KILL: any supplied off-limits term or default off-limits hit (disease verbs, drug-name adjacency, body-transformation framing, weight-loss audience triggers, *guarantee* / *cure* / *prevent* without legal sign-off) = rewrite the line, rescan from the top.
1. **H1 strength.** KILL: any H1 that is a saturated DTC opener (*"The future of X"*, *"X, reimagined"*) or could belong to any brand in the category. KILL: two H1s on the same angle; replace one.
2. **ATF sufficiency.** KILL: an ATF (H1 + subhead + value prop) that doesn't tell the buyer what it is, who it's for, and why now before they scroll. If they must scroll to learn what the product is, 60%+ of mobile traffic is gone.
3. **Scannability.** KILL: a wall of prose past the fold; any value-prop paragraph over 5 sentences; bullets that don't end in a specific.
4. **Proof placement and quality.** KILL: proof buried below the FAQ; testimonials over 15 words; press callouts that aren't real and recognizable; before/after framed as body change.
5. **CTA stack.** KILL: no primary CTA above the fold; a CTA missing verb + object + link; competing asks at the same decision point.
6. **Voice authenticity.** KILL: any em dash, template cadence, corporate vocabulary (*passion, dedication, excellence*). Read aloud — does it match the brand's founder or Instagram voice?
7. **Saturated phrase audit.** KILL: *"Game changer"*, *"Holy grail"*, *"Obsessed"*, *"As seen on"* (without real press), *"Doctor approved"* (without a named doctor), *"Clean ingredients"*, *"Made with love"*, *"Backed by science"* (without a citation).

**Ship bar:** a cold mobile visitor reading only the ATF can say what the product is, who it's for, and why now in 5 seconds; every module earns its scroll; every claim has a number, name, or mechanism behind it. If a remaining fix would take under a minute, the draft is not done.

Do NOT print the critique on first drafts. When to print it: same exceptions as `dr-script` (user asks, audit mode, post-approval revision, "go deep"). When to skip: "no critique" / single-module iteration (critique only the changed module).

## Page-goal nuance

Different LP goals need different module priorities:

- **Cold-traffic acquisition** — H1 + subhead + value prop + proof above the fold; FAQ heavy; primary CTA repeated 3+ times.
- **Warm lifecycle / retargeting** — assume buyer knows the brand; lead with new offer / urgency / restock / variant; less explanation, more CTA.
- **PDP (product detail page)** — bullet-heavy, ingredient/spec table, single primary CTA, fewer testimonials (rely on review widget).
- **Advertorial / long-form sales letter** — narrative-led, story-as-pitch, problem-amplification + mechanism + proof + offer; CTA only after the emotional payoff.

Match module weight to the goal.

## Failure modes — do not repeat

- **Full-page dump on a module ask.** User asks for "the H1" and gets eight modules. Single-module requests get only that module.
- **Markdown creep.** `#` headers, bold module names, boxed callouts, or fences in the output. Module name on its own plain line, prose under it.
- **Interchangeable H1s.** Five H1s that are the same claim reworded, or H1s any competitor could run. Each must test a distinct angle and carry the DSI.
- **Page-goal blindness.** Writing a cold-traffic module stack for a PDP or advertorial. Goal drives module weight; ask if unknown.
- **FAQ filler.** "What is X?" and "How do I order?" questions that block nothing. Only real purchase-blocking objections.
- **Fake proof.** Press strips without real press, "doctor approved" without a doctor, invented testimonial names. Proof that can't survive a screenshot gets cut.
- **Body-change before/after.** Transformation framing in proof or bullets. Behavior change only, and only if brand-approved.
- **Smuggled canon.** Reusing another brand's stats or CTA (especially Shameless's 26g/70/3/3) as filler for a new brand. Placeholders marked, never borrowed.
- **Critique leakage / em dashes.** Same as `dr-script`: corrected artifact only; no em dashes in copy.

## Output discipline checklist

Before printing, verify:

- ✅ Modules in order, separated by blank lines, labeled with module name on its own line.
- ✅ No fences, no markdown headers (`#`), no boxed callouts, no em dashes.
- ✅ 5 numbered H1 options span distinct angles, not five rewrites.
- ✅ Subhead complements H1, doesn't paraphrase.
- ✅ Value prop covers what + who + why-different + after-state.
- ✅ Bullets start with benefit verb, end with specific.
- ✅ Proof block format matches brand and page goal.
- ✅ FAQ tackles real objections, not "What is X?" filler.
- ✅ Primary CTA = verb + object + link, named clearly.
- ✅ Brand canon honored if supplied; default off-limits respected; placeholders marked if canon missing.
- ✅ Internal critique checklist run in order, every kill-criterion resolved, critique not printed (unless asked).
- ✅ Post-LP: ask follow-up questions to drive toward approval (which H1 to lock, page goal, primary CTA target).
